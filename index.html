Integrative Microbiology, Multi-Omics & Applied Bioinformatics

Making microbial systems visible, testable and traceable.

CLARITY LAB at UCD, led by Dr Guerrino Macori, integrates culture-dependent microbiology, direct-from-sample sequencing, multi-omics, emerging diagnostic tools and open bioinformatics to study microbial diversity, food safety and biological risk across the food chain, built environments, animals and human health contexts.

Explainer

What is microbial dark matter?

Many microorganisms remain difficult to grow, classify or interpret using standard methods. We study this hidden microbial diversity by combining culture-dependent microbiology, direct-from-sample sequencing, multi-omics and bioinformatics.

This allows us to investigate not only which microbes are present, but what they may be doing, how they interact and why they matter for food safety, antimicrobial resistance and One Health.

Research

Research pillars

Our work connects microbial discovery, food-system safety, pathogen genomics, antimicrobial resistance and reproducible analysis to generate insight for public health and safer food systems.

01

Microbial Dark Matter & Model Microbiomes

We investigate hidden and poorly characterised microorganisms in complex microbial communities, using fermented foods and other model systems to understand microbial diversity, function and resilience.

02

Food Safety & Microbial Ecology

We study microbial communities across food systems to understand safety, quality, spoilage, persistence and risk.

03

Pathogen Genomics, AMR & One Health

We use microbiological, genomic and bioinformatic tools to track pathogens, antimicrobial resistance and food-chain risks across human, animal, environmental and built-environment contexts.

04

Methods, Multi-Omics & Open Bioinformatics

We develop and apply reproducible workflows to analyse complex microbial, genomic and multi-omics data, supporting microbial ecology, food safety and pathogen surveillance.

Principal Investigator

Dr Guerrino Macori

Assistant Professor, UCD School of Biology and Environmental Science

Dr Guerrino Macori leads CLARITY LAB at UCD. He is an Assistant Professor in the UCD School of Biology and Environmental Science and a Conway Fellow, with additional affiliations to the UCD Centre for Food Safety and the UCD Institute of Food and Health.

His research examines microbial systems relevant to food safety, public health and One Health. The lab brings together food microbiology, microbial ecology, pathogen surveillance and applied bioinformatics to study how microbial communities are structured, how risks emerge and how biological evidence can be interpreted clearly.

Guerrino also teaches and supervises in areas related to applied bioinformatics, microbial genomics and microbiome analysis. His group provides a training environment for students and researchers working across experimental and computational microbiology.
