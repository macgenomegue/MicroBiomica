Integrative Microbiology, Multi-Omics & Applied Bioinformatics

Making microbial systems visible, testable and traceable.

CLARITY LAB at UCD studies microbial systems that affect food safety, public health, antimicrobial resistance and One Health.

We combine culture-based microbiology, direct-from-sample sequencing, long-read genomics, multi-omics and reproducible bioinformatics to make complex microbial communities easier to detect, interpret and act on.

Explainer

What is microbial dark matter?

Many microbial systems remain poorly characterised. This includes organisms that are difficult to culture, complex microbiomes, mobile genetic elements, antimicrobial resistance genes and microbial interactions that are not visible using standard approaches.

CLARITY LAB investigates these systems by connecting laboratory microbiology with computational analysis.

Our work asks:

  • Which microorganisms are present?
  • What functions do they carry?
  • How do pathogens, plasmids and resistance genes move?
  • Which microbial patterns indicate risk?
  • How can sequencing and bioinformatics support clearer decisions?

Research

Research pillars

Our work connects microbial discovery, food-system safety, pathogen genomics, antimicrobial resistance and reproducible analysis to generate insight for public health and safer food systems.

01

Microbial dark matter & model microbiomes

We study hidden and poorly characterised microbial diversity in complex communities. Fermented foods, model microbiomes and environmental systems are used to investigate microbial function, resilience and interaction.

02

Food safety & microbial ecology

We examine microbial communities across food systems to understand safety, spoilage, persistence and microbial risk.

03

Pathogen genomics, AMR & One Health

We use genome sequencing, metagenomics and bioinformatics to study pathogens, antimicrobial resistance and biological risk across food, animal, human, environmental and built-environment contexts.

04

Methods, multi-omics & open bioinformatics

We develop and apply reproducible workflows for microbial genomics, microbiome analysis, long-read sequencing and multi-omics integration.

Current research areas

• Long-read sequencing for microbial identification and surveillance
• Metagenomics for antimicrobial resistance monitoring
• Microbial dark matter in food and environmental systems
• Taxonomy-free and AI-supported analysis of microbial communities
• Pathogen genomics and genomic epidemiology
• Fermented foods as model microbial ecosystems
• Reproducible bioinformatics workflows for research and training

Principal Investigator

Dr Guerrino Macori

Assistant Professor, UCD School of Biology and Environmental Science
Principal Investigator, CLARITY LAB at UCD

Dr Guerrino Macori leads research in applied bioinformatics, food microbiology, pathogen genomics and microbiome analysis. His work focuses on microbial systems relevant to food safety, public health and One Health.

The lab integrates experimental and computational approaches to study microbial communities, biological risk, antimicrobial resistance and the interpretation of complex sequencing data.

Guerrino teaches and supervises students in applied bioinformatics, pathogen genomics, food safety and microbiome analysis.

Sara Benetti

Pathogens and spoilers persist in food environments, posing challenges for food safety

Sara’s research investigates how foodborne pathogens and spoilage bacteria persist in food-processing environments despite exposure to cleaning, processing and other stress conditions. Her project focuses on bacterial dormancy as a possible survival strategy, using microbiology and molecular biology approaches to explore the pathways that allow persistent bacteria to withstand stress and reappear over time.
